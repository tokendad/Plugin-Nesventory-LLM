Christmas In The City
Revised 10/29/2022

The Christmas In The City Village series is a porcelain collection that falls under the Heritage Village Collection umbrella and was originally designed by Neilan Lund. Buildings in this collection, in the beginning, were said to be inspired by a typical metropolitan “Big City”. As time has evolved, it has grown to encompass actual buildings in New York City as well as landmarks in Chicago and Boston.

The original set of three buildings was issued under one SKU number with one for the entire set. By coincidence or design, a hint to the new village series came with the release of the 1986 accessory Lighted Tree With Children & Ladder (s/3) which referenced Christmas In The City on its sleeve.

Christmas In The City is home to subseries such as Chinatown, St. Patrick's Day, the port of East Harbor, Restaurants, Row Houses, as well as licensing with Coca-Cola and Harley-Davidson.

The concept for Christmas in The City was inspired by a major metropolitan city. The collection is made of porcelain and reflects buildings one would, traditionally, find in such a place. For many years collectors tried to determine if New York was the true inspiration for the collection. In later years, actual buildings/bridges and other iconic structures from there and other major cities were incorporated. The first indication of Christmas In The City came in the form of an accessory that was introduced one year prior to the village's official release.
